# Neural Transmission Sandbox

A browser-based interactive visualization of how neurons
work — from a single cell to a firing network.

No download. No install. Open it and fire a signal
through your own understanding of how you think.

## What It Is

Every thought you have ever had started as an
electrochemical signal crossing a 20-nanometer gap.

Every mood. Every memory. Every decision. Every
sensation of pain or pleasure or recognition.
All of it is neurons following simple rules about
when to fire and when to stay quiet.

Neural Transmission Sandbox makes those rules visible,
manipulable, and personally meaningful.

## Tiers

**Tier I — The Neuron**
A single neuron, fully alive on canvas. Dendrites
sway and branch. The soma pulses. Myelin sheaths
glow along the axon. Nodes of Ranvier pulse between
them. Synaptic terminals drift with vesicles orbiting
inside. Sodium, potassium, and chloride ions float
in the background — the raw ingredients of every
signal your body has ever sent.

Click any structure for its function and — more
importantly — why it has the shape it does.
The long axon exists because distance is its job.
The myelin sheath exists because evolution needed
faster signals without thicker axons. Every
millimeter of structure is about signal velocity.

**Tier II — The Action Potential**
Set a stimulus. Watch what threshold means.

Below threshold: nothing happens. At threshold:
the membrane fires completely. Not partially.
Not proportionally. Fully. Every time.

This is the all-or-nothing law — one of the most
elegant design principles in biology. A stronger
stimulus does not produce a stronger signal.
It produces the same signal, faster.

Watch the depolarization wave travel the axon.
Sodium floods in at the front. Potassium follows
behind. The refractory zone locks behind the wave
so the signal can only travel one direction.

Then experiment:
- Drop myelination and watch the conduction
  velocity collapse from 70 m/s to near zero
- Lower temperature and watch the signal slow
- Block sodium channels and watch the action
  potential fail entirely

That last one is labeled. It is exactly how
local anesthetics work. It is how some of the
most dangerous neurotoxins on Earth work.
Understanding it takes about thirty seconds here.

A live voltage graph draws membrane potential
in real time at the bottom of the canvas.

**Tier III — The Synaptic Cleft**
The synaptic cleft is 20 nanometers wide.
Your entire emotional life happens in that gap.

Watch vesicles release neurotransmitters from the
presynaptic terminal. Watch them cross the cleft,
bind receptors, and change the postsynaptic
membrane potential in real time.

Switch between four neurotransmitters:

- Glutamate — primary excitatory, fires the next
  neuron, learning and memory depend on it
- GABA — primary inhibitory, the brain's natural
  brake, alcohol mimics it
- Dopamine — reward and motivation, Parkinson's
  is its depletion, addiction hijacks its pathway
- Serotonin — mood and sleep regulation, 90% of
  it lives in your gut, not your brain

Then adjust reuptake. Drop it to zero and watch
neurotransmitters flood the cleft, signal strength
increase, duration extend. This is the exact
mechanism of every SSRI antidepressant ever made.
Not a side effect. Not a theory. The mechanism,
visible, in the gap between two cells.

Adjust receptor sensitivity and watch up-regulation
and down-regulation happen in real time. These are
the mechanisms behind tolerance and withdrawal.

**Tier IV — The Network**
Scale out to a full neural network. Excitatory
and inhibitory neurons. Adjustable count,
connectivity, and inhibitory ratio.

Click any neuron to stimulate it and watch the
cascade propagate through the network. Then:

- Strengthen pathways by firing them repeatedly —
  neurons that fire together wire together,
  Hebb's rule, the basis of learning and memory
- Weaken inactive pathways — use it or lose it,
  the basis of forgetting and skill atrophy
- Damage a neuron and watch connections sever
  and the network attempt to reroute around
  the loss
- Observe the coherence meter — random firing
  scores low, coordinated pathway activation
  scores high, the difference between noise
  and thought

The network in Tier IV is a simplified model
of what is happening in your head right now.
86 billion neurons. 100 trillion connections.
The pattern of activity across them is the
thought you are currently thinking.

## The Salt and Water Connection
Ion channels are the mechanism of neural
transmission. Sodium flows in. Potassium flows out.
The balance of electrolytes in your body — sodium,
potassium, chloride — is not just about hydration.
It is the literal voltage source for every signal
your nervous system sends.

Too little sodium: signals weaken.
Too much: neurons become hyperexcitable.
The same is true for water balance, temperature,
and blood oxygen. Tier II lets you feel all of this
directly. The sliders are not abstract — they are
the variables your body manages every second to
keep you thinking.
